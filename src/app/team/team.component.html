The Pluvial Company

We develop web and mobile precision farming apps that are based on satellite imagery and machine learning technologies.

OneSoil began as an initiative between two friends. The co-founders, Usevalad Henin and Slava Mazai, met in Minsk in 2014. In the beginning, they did custom orders for farmers. Slava would take pictures from a drone, and Usevalad would make maps for the variable-rate application (VRA) of fertilizers based on their analysis.

In 2016, Sasha Yakovlev became the third co-founder. The team began using machine learning algorithms to process field data, and the company expanded to six people. Everyone worked for free. For a year and a half, no one took a salary. At the end of 2017, the company received its first investment. This allowed the team to work faster to create the free OneSoil apps that launched in the summer of 2018.
